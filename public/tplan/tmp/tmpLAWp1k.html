Teacher Notes
  • This lesson acts as an introduction to apparatus
  • The aim of the lesson is to teach how apparatus can be used and the safety rules that must be followed when using it. The children should be introduced to the following types of apparatus:
  • Mats - used for performing actions such as balances and rolls. Also positioned around apparatus as a safety measure
  • Benches - can be travelled along, over; jumped on and off
  • Tables/Stools - can be travelled along, over, under; jumped on and off depending on the height
  • Hand apparatus - e.g. Balls, beanbags, hoops, quoits can be used to throw, catch, dribble, move with
  • The children must be taught the rules of safe apparatus use - to help reduce the risk of injuries or accidents. Below are some basic rules that can be adopted, but each environment must set appropriate rules for their surrounding:
  • Mats must be position safely around all apparatus where it is possible to work off the ground
  • All apparatus must be positioned safely in sufficient space, away from other apparatus, equipment and walls
  • Tables/Stools - only one child per time can stand on a table/stool. Rules set about dismounting each table/stool depending on the height e.g. High tables can only be climbed down from, low tables can be jumped off
  • Mats must be used to perform actions such as balancing and rolling
  • Hand apparatus - there is sufficient space to perform the actions safely
  • All apparatus must be used in a safe and controlled manner at all times
  • If jumping on or off apparatus the children must first be taught/reminded how to land safely:
  • Land with feet together
  • Land on toes, then press the heels into the floor
  • Head looking forward
  • Knees slightly bent - up to 90 degrees
  • Back straight
  • Arms straight in front of body - this encourages good posture
  • Hold the landing position (see diagram) for 3 seconds before standing up
  • All apparatus must be safety checked before it is used. It must be set up safely and checked before the children are allowed to use it
  • The children should be encourage to explore the different pieces of apparatus within the lesson and experience using them in a safe and sensible way

Skill DevelopmentHow to Land - diagramStrand : ADDuration : 3 - 5Teaching Points
  • Ask the children to find a space to work in
  • Ask the children to perform a small jump in their space and land with the following technique:
  •     Land with feet together
  •     Land on the balls of the feet, then press heels into the floor
  •     Head facing forward
  •     Knees slightly bent - up to 90 degrees
  •     Back straight
  •     Arms straight in front of body - this encourages good posture
  •     Hold the landing position (see diagram) for 3 seconds before standing up
  • Ask the children to keep practicing their landing technique
  • Check that all the children can perform a landing correctly before moving onto further activities
  • Explain that it is important to land properly to reduce the risk of injury
  • Ensure the children understand and perform each of the steps to land safely

Skill DevelopmentJumping off Apparatus - diagramStrand : ADDuration : 5 - 10Teaching Points
  • The children must previously have been taught how to land correctly - see 'How to Land' activity
  • Spread mats around the area - ensure they are safely spaced
  • Ask the children to find a mat to work on - ideally 2 children to a mat; the children work as shown in the diagram. The children can work in larger groups if there are not enough mats, with 2 children working at a time and the group taking turns
  • The aim of the activity is to teach the children how to jump off apparatus safely - this must only be performed off low apparatus when there is appropriate matting in place
  • Step 1: Standing on the mat ask the children to perform a small jump showing the correct landing technique - feet together; land on toes, then press heels into the floor; head facing forwards; knees slightly bent; back straight; arms straight out in front of body; hold for 3 seconds
  • Step 2: Standing on the mat ask the children to perform a small jump onto the floor
  • Step 3: Place a second mat on top of the first to create a higher step. Again standing on the mats ask the children to jump onto the floor. This may require groups to join together and take turns
  • Step 4: Introduce a third mat to increase the jump height. Again groups may need to join together and take turns
  • Ensure the children are confident and performing the jumps with a good technique before progressing to the next step
  • Ensure mats are not damaged
  • Ensure mats are placed firmly and directly on top of each other
  • Only perform small jumps - the aim of the activity it to develop the correct technique
  • Encourage the children to bend knees and push off the floor when jumping
  • Use the arms, swing them up, as jumping
  • Ensure the children use the correct landing technique
  • Take turns when sharing mats
To make activity harder
  • Introduce the steps more quickly
To make activity easier
  • Introduce the steps gradually

RoutineCreating a Routine - BenchStrand : ADDuration : 10 - 20Teaching Points
  • Spread all available benches around the room - ensure there is appropriate matting around each bench
  • Taking turns to work on the benches ask the children to create a routine that travels along the bench
  • The routine should include:
  •     A start position, on the floor, at one end of the bench
  •     Stepping onto the bench, a balance, a way of travelling along the bench, a shape and a way of getting off the bench
  •     A finish position, on the floor, at the other end of the bench
  • Once the children have developed their routine ask them to keep practicing and improving it to make it the best it can be
  • The children that are not working on the bench can create/practice/refine their routine whilst waiting for their turn to work on the bench
  • Encourage the children to perform the skills to the best of their ability
  • Ensure the correct technique is used to perform all elements/skills
  • Encourage the children to show control over the skills
  • Ensure floor mats are surrounding the bench area
  • Highlight and show the use of good technique
  • Provide feedback on how the routine could be improved
  • SAFETY POINT: Ensure all actions are performed safely and mats are used where necessary
  • Ensure the children can perform the balance and shapes safely and correctly on the floor first
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Once the children have developed a routine ask some of the children to perform theirs for the rest of the class to see
  • Encourage the audience to give feedback - What did they like about the performance?
