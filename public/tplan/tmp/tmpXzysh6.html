Teacher Notes
  • Dribbling is the ability to move with the ball under control. This means keeping the ball close to the head of the hockey stick
  • The aim of the lesson is to develop the children's ability to move with the ball under increasing control using the hockey stick
  • When dribbling the hockey stick should be held with the left hand at the top of the handle and the right hand near the middle of the stick
  • Encourage the children to keep their heads up to look where they are going when dribbling

Skill DevelopmentThrough the Gate - Dribbling - diagramStrand : ADDuration : 5 - 10Teaching Points
  • Set out 25 - 30 gates (Using 2 marker cones, 2 steps apart)
  • Each child will need a hockey stick and 1 ball
  • The children must dribble their ball around the area and through as many gates as possible
  • Encourage the children to speed up gradually but keep the ball under control
  • Keep your ball under control
  • Move slowly to start with and keep control
  • Move to free gates
  • Change direction to move from gate to gate
  • Hold your stick in 2 hands at all times when dribbling
  • Try to keep the ball in contact with your stick
  • Keep your head up to avoid collision when moving around
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • How many gates can you move through in 1 minute?
  • Ask the children to repeat the activity with the aim of beating their previous score
  • Must go to a different gate every time
  • Keep your ball under control
  • Call out coloured sequence of gates the children must pass through in order
  • Encourage the children to keep their heads up to look where they are going
To make activity harder
  • Make the gates narrower
To make activity easier
  • Make the gates wider

Skill DevelopmentCriss-Cross Relay - diagramStrand : ADDuration : 5 - 10Teaching Points
  • Split the class into groups of 10
  • Set out a 10 by 10 step area
  • Each child will need a hockey stick and each group 2 balls
  • Split the group evenly (if possible) into 4 smaller groups and position one group at each corner of the square
  • The groups will be working with the group diagonally opposite them
  • The first child dribbles the ball in a diagonal line across the square to their team-mate on the other side and gives them the ball before joining the back of their line
  • Both groups go at the same time so the children are crossing each other in the middle of the square
  • Change speed or direction to avoid collisions in the middle of the area
  • Dribble the ball at the '2 o'clock' position
  • Keep the ball in contact with the stick as much as possible
  • Keep your head up and look at your team-mate you are moving towards
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Increase the size of the area to 15 by 15 steps
  • Ask the children to dribble the ball just past half way across and perform a pass whilst on the move to the waiting team-mate at the opposite side
  • Follow your pass and join the back of the line
  • When receiving a pass move to the line of the ball and keep stick on the floor
  • When passing on the move keep body momentum going forward and push through the line of the ball
  • Watch the ball onto the end of your stick
  • Follow through to help aim the pass in the right direction
  • Angle of the face of the stick towards the floor
To make activity harder
  • Speed up the activity - make sure the ball is kept under control
To make activity easier
  • Slow down the activity - ask the children to walk

GameKnock out - diagramStrand : AD SADuration : 10 - 20Teaching Points
  • Split the class into groups of 9
  • Set up a 20 by 20 step area for each group to play on - each area has only one goal as shown in the diagram
  • In each group select one child to be the Goalkeeper
  • The remaining 8 children play against each other
  • The aim of the game is for each child to score a goal
  • When they score a goal the child progresses to the next round - they must stand at the side of the pitch and wait for the round to be completed
  • The round is completed when the 7th child scores a goal to progress to the next round
  • The child that did not score is out of the game - they can become the referee in round 2
  • Round 2 then starts with 7 children
  • Keep repeating the process with the child failing to score eliminated from each round - they can have a turn at refereeing the next round
  • The 'final' takes place when there are only two children remaining - the first child to score 2 goals in the final is the winner
  • Highlight successful play within the games and demonstrate it to the children
  • Shoot when you have a good sight of goal
  • Use dribbling skills
  • Aim shots
  • Play fairly - follow the rules
  • SAFETY POINT: Use a suitable - SOFT ball that will not hurt the goalkeeper
  • SAFETY POINT: Use the hockey sticks safely and sensibly
  • Use individual skills to play the game
To make activity harder
  • A child must score two goals to progress through to the next round
To make activity easier
  • Play the game with no goalkeepers
