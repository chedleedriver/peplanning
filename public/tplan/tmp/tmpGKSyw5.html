Teacher Notes
  • The aim of the lesson is to develop the childrens ability to link shapes together
  • Linking shapes is the first step in developing the childrens understanding and ability to create gymnastics sequences
  • Encourage the children to experiment and create their own ideas of how shapes can be linked together
  • The children should consider the following points when linking shapes:
  • The linking action e.g. travel, balance, roll
  • Level - the level used to link the shapes
  • Speed - the speed of the transition between shapes
  • Encourage the children to perform all shapes and actions with quality:
  • Showing a clear beginning and end to each shape
  • With smooth transitions between shapes

Warm UpTraffic LightsStrand : KUFHDuration : 3 - 5Teaching Points
  • The children should travel around the area reacting to the following signals as they are called out by the teachers:
  •     Red = Stop freeze where you are
  •     Amber = Get ready and jump/jog on the spot
  •     Green = Go and travel around the area. Keep varying the way the children travel
  • Head up to look where you are going
  • Take care not to bump into anyone
  • Vary the way of travelling e.g. hopping, skipping, jumping

Coordination and MovementFunny WalksStrand : ADDuration : 3 - 5Teaching Points
  • Set out a 30 by 30 step area for the children to work in
  • Each child should work on their own and move around the area
  • Ask the children to think of different ways they can walk/move
  • Let the children have a few movements to experiment and try some of their own ideas
  • Ask one of the children to show the rest of the class their idea - once they have shown the class ask everyone else if they can copy what they have seen
  • Keep asking children to show the class and then asking the rest of the class to copy what they have seen
  • Encourage the children to think of different ideas and ways of moving
  • Watch carefully and try to copy the exact action/movement you seen
  • Move into space
  • Head up to look where you are going
  • Start with slower movements/actions and gradually increase the speed
To make activity harder
  • Speed the activity up
To make activity easier

Skill DevelopmentKey Shapes - diagramStrand : ADDuration : 10 - 15Teaching Points
  • Spread mats around the area - ensure they are spaced out safely
  • Ask the children to find a space on a mat to work on their own, ideally one or two children to a mat
  • Teach the children the following key gymnastics shapes - See notes below about each of the key shapes and relevant diagrams:
  •     Straight - Standing with feet together. Head facing forwards. Reach arms up to the ceiling, shoulder width apart. Ensure all the body is in a straight line. Point the hands spreading the fingers
  •     Tuck - Sitting with bottom on the floor. Feet and knees kept together. Keeping back straight, bend legs bringing knees up to chest. Place chin on chest. Ensure the legs are tucked into the body as tightly as possible
  •     Star - Standing up with feet apart. Place arms straight out to the side of the body. Imagine legs and arms being pulled apart to create the shape of a star. Ensure the legs and arms are as straight as possible. Point the hands
  •     Dish - Lying on the back with body in a straight shape. Lift head slightly off the floor to look at toes. Lift the feet slightly off the floor about the same height as the head, keeping the legs straight and leaving just the back in contact with the floor. Rest hands on top of legs. To make it easier bend legs to start off with. Ensure the back is pushed into the floor so there is no gap between the lower back and floor. Point the toes and hands
  •     Arch - Lying on the front of the body in a straight shape. Keeping arms and legs straight. Lift head, arms and legs off the floor, keeping legs and feet together leaving just the stomach in contact with the floor. Ensure the arms and legs are only lifted slightly off the floor so the children do not bend their back too much. Point the toes and hands
  •     Pike - Sit on the floor with legs and feet placed out in front of the body. Keep legs and back straight with legs and feet placed firmly together. Keep arms straight and place hands on legs. Point the toes and hands
  •     Straddle - Sitting on the floor with legs wide apart. Keep legs straight and the whole leg in contact with the floor. Point toes. Place arms out to the side of the body in line with the legs. Keep back straight and look forward. Ensure the legs and arms are as straight as possible. Point the toes and hands
  • Talk through and demonstrate each of the key points for the shapes
  • Encourage the children to perform the shapes to the best of their ability
  • Encourage the children to use correct technique
  • Select and highlight good quality shapes
  • Show good body tension when performing each shape
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Challenge the children to remember the names of the shapes
  • Ask the children to travel around the room - call out the shapes for the children to perform
  • The children must perform the correct shape as quickly as possible
  • Select and highlight good quality shapes
  • Remind the children of the key points for each shape
To make activity harder
  • Use a larger variety of shapes to remember
To make activity easier

AnalysisTo recognise and describe what their bodies feel like during different types of activity - KUFH Level 2
  • Discuss with the children changes they feel when doing different types of activity i.e. slow controlled activities (balances), warm-ups, faster movement activities (sequences)
  • Ask the children to take part in the activities as planned and to think about how they feel during the lesson
  • Before each activity ask the children to predict changes to their body they think will happen when they do the activity. Encourage the children to think about - Heart rate, breathing rate and body temperature
  • After each activity ask the children to describe the changes they have noticed to their body
  • Did you correctly predict how your body would change?
  • Repeat this process before and after each new activity within the lesson

SequenceLink Together Different Actions - Travelling and Shapes - diagramStrand : ADDuration : 10 - 15Teaching Points
  • Ask the children to create a sequence linking together different ways of travelling and shapes they have learnt in the lesson (or in previous lessons)
  • The sequence should include:
  •     Linking together 2 ways of travelling and 2 shapes. They can be arranged and performed in any order
  • Make sure the actions are performed with quality
  • Encourage and highlight the use of good technique
  • Encourage smooth transition between the different actions
  • Encourage the children to show control over the movements and actions
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Once the children have developed a routine ask some of the children to perform theirs for the rest of the class
  • Encourage the audience to give feedback - What did they like about the performance?
To make activity harder
  • Increase the number of elements to be linked
To make activity easier
  • Reduce the number of elements to be linked

Warm DownCircle Stretch - diagramStrand : KUFHDuration : 2 - 5Teaching Points
  • Split the class into groups of 6
  • Give each group a ball and ask them to form a small circle
  • Each child should stand with their feet shoulder width apart and reach down so their hands hang near the ground
  • The ball is then passed around the group - children should roll it around the circle
  • Ask the children to roll the ball around the circle 3 times in one direction and then 3 times in the opposite direction
  • Encourage children to keep their legs straight
  • Gradually decrease intensity

Plenary
What does it mean to link shapes together?
  • Performing one shape after another to create a sequence, other actions can also be linked to the shapes e.g. rolls, travelling actions, balances
What examples of linking shapes can you think of?
  • Encourage the children to think of examples they created in the lesson and explain or demonstrate them e.g. perform a tuck shape followed by a star shape
What do you need to show when performing each key shape?
  • A clear beginning and end to each shape; Stretch before and after each shape; Control; Balance
