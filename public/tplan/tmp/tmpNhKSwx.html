Warm UpStuck in the Mud - Football - diagramStrand : KUFH ADDuration : 5 - 10Teaching Points
  • Select 3 children to be ON
  • Give the rest of the children 1 ball each and ask them to dribble it around the area
  • The 3 children that are ON move around the area and tag the dribblers
  • If a child is tagged they must stop, stand still with their legs apart, holding the ball in two hands
  • A child is freed when another dribbler who has not been tagged passes their ball between their legs. They can then put their ball on the ground and join back in with the game
  • Change the children who are on every few minutes
  • Move away from taggers
  • Use turns to keep away from taggers
  • Head up to see where you are going
  • Keep control of your ball
  • Move quickly - but keep control of your ball
To make activity harder
  • Introduce more taggers
To make activity easier
  • Have fewer taggers

Skill DevelopmentSkill Circuit - diagramStrand : AD SADuration : 10 - 15Teaching Points
  • Split the class into small groups of 3-5 children
  • Set up several stations around the area to make a circuit - there needs to be enough stations for every group
  • Select stations that are appropriate for the ability of the children in your class
  • The following are suggestions for circuits - they can be repeated if necessary:
  • * Pass the ball - split the small group into pairs - each pair has a ball and passes back and forth between one another
  • * Keep it up - each player has 1 ball and completes as many kick-ups as they can
  • * Keep it up with the head - each player has 1 ball and completes as many keep-ups as they can using their head
  • * Dribble - each player has 1 ball and must complete as many lengths dribbling the ball as they can
  • * Keep the ball - the group is given 1 ball between them. One player is selected to be ON - the rest must keep the ball from this player. If the player gets the ball, they swap places with the last player to touch the ball
  • * Through the target - set out small gates with marker cones for the ball to be passed back and forth through
  • * Slalom - set up a line of 7 marker cones. Each child has to dribble the ball down the slalom weaving in and out of the cones, then return to start and repeat
  • Can you think of any other stations you could introduce?
  • Change stations after 2 minutes
  • Encourage and highlight good control of the ball
  • Follow the instructions on each station
  • Use appropriate skills at each station
To make activity harder
  • Pick appropriately challenging stations
To make activity easier
  • Pick appropriately challenging stations

GameInvasion Games - Unit 24 - Final Lesson - diagramStrand : AD SA EIDuration : 15 - 20Teaching Points
  • The aim of the game is to beat the opposition by scoring more goals
  • Play the game four against four and then five against five
  • Play on a pitch that is 15 to 20 steps wide, and 30 to 40 steps long
  • Position one goal at each end of the pitch, with a shooting area of about 5 steps by 5 steps around it
  • At first, play without goalkeepers and only allow players to shoot when they are in the shooting area
  • Later, allow players to shoot from outside the area and add goalkeepers
  • Encourage teams to think about how they need to change the way they organise themselves to attack and defend in the different games
  • Adaptations and variations on the task
  • Vary the game a) with only attackers allowed in the shooting area. b) with one defender and any number of attackers allowed in the area. c) with nobody allowed in the area
  • Play on a pitch with a larger or smaller shooting area
  • Play the game with only a limited number of players on each side allowed to score goals
  • Play with uneven sides, e.g. five against three or five against four, to make it easier for some children than others
  • Play with smaller goals, or only have a limited number of touches (for more able children)
  • Play with bigger goals, more goals at one end, or fewer players so they have more time (for less able children)
  • Explain the rules and how the game works then let the children explore the game for themselves
  • Let the children have a go at the game
  • Observe children whilst playing the game - are they able to play it successfully?
  • Provide advice and guidance if necessary
  • What skills do you need to use? Ask the children to think about this as they play the game
  • Experiment with different ideas and skills you have practised throughout the unit
  • Ask the children if they can remember the rules of the game from last time they played it
  • Have the children improved since they last played the game? Are they more successful
  • Encourage the children to think about the tactics they have learnt throughout the unit
