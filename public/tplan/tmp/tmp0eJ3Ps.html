Teacher Notes
  • Fielding is the action of a player (fielder)/groups of players (fielders) performed after the batter has hit the ball, it involves using skills such as stopping, gathering and collecting the ball, catching and throwing skills
  • The aim when fielding is to stop the batters from scoring runs, the best way to do this is to get them out
  • The lesson should focus on learning and developing basic fielding skills such as stopping, collecting and gathering a ball, catching and throwing skills

Warm UpFocus on the armsStrand : KUFHDuration : 2 - 5Teaching Points
  • Ask the children to move around a 20 by 20 step area - start walking
  • Ask the children if they can think of different ways of moving?
  • Before starting to move in a different way all the children should stop in a space and make arm circles
  • Repeat this each time before moving in a different way - perform arm circles of different sizes and speeds each time
  • Repeat this 5/6 times moving in different ways each time
  • Gradually build up the speed of the activity
  • Move around area with head up - look where you are going
  • Move into space
  • Make sure you are in a space to perform arm circles
  • Straight arms when making circles

Skill DevelopmentCatch in Different Ways - PairsStrand : ADDuration : 2 - 5Teaching Points
  • The children work in pairs with 1 ball between them
  • They must stand facing each other 3/4 steps apart
  • The ball should be thrown back and forth between the pairs
  • The child who receives the ball must catch it in the following ways
  • 1 - Catch the ball in two hands
  • 2 - Catch the ball with one hand
  • 3 - Alternate one handed catches - first catch with right hand, next - left, next - right and so on
  • Progress the children when they are ready to move on
  • Keep your eyes on the ball
  • Hand(s) ready to catch
  • Move if necessary to get in line with the ball
  • Good quality throws to partner
To make activity harder
  • Move the children closer together, there is less time to react to make a successful catch
To make activity easier
  • Move the children further apart, there is more time to react to make a successful catch

Skill DevelopmentThe Long Barrier - diagramStrand : ADDuration : 5 - 10 Teaching Points
  • The Long Barrier is a fielding technique used to stop the ball
  • Demonstrate the technique for the long barrier to the children - ask them to copy you as you perform and talk them through the steps
  • The children should then work in pairs with 1 ball between them in space
  • The pairs should stand 8 - 10 steps apart facing each other and roll the ball back and forth to each other using the long barrier technique to stop the ball each time
  • Move to get in line with the ball as it comes towards you
  • Get into the position as shown in the diagram in line with the ball
  • Always attempt to use the hands to stop the ball first - it is then easy to throw quickly
  • The legs form a barrier to stop the ball in case it is missed with the hands
  • Keep eyes on the ball
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Repeat the activity but rolling the ball slightly to the side of your partner
  • Watch the ball
  • Use the long barrier technique to stop the ball
  • Move to get in line with the ball as it comes towards you
  • Move quickly to get into position
To make activity harder
  • Increase the distance to be rolled
  • Use a smaller, harder ball
  • Throw the ball back to partner underarm so the ball may bounce slightly
  • Vary the pace of the throw to your partner
To make activity easier
  • Decrease the distance to be rolled
  • Use a larger, softer ball

Skill DevelopmentHit the Cone - diagramStrand : ADDuration : 5 - 10 Teaching Points
  • The game is played in pairs
  • Each pair requires 1 ball and a cone
  • The children should stand facing each other 8 - 10 steps apart with the cone positioned in the middle between the two children
  • The aim of the game is to roll the ball to hit the cone in the middle
  • Each time a child hits the cone they score 1 point
  • Keep rolling the ball back and forth keeping score of the game
  • Roll the ball underarm
  • Keep your arm straight
  • Swing arm straight back and follow through
  • Aim roll towards the cone
  • Keep score of the game
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Play the game for one minute - how many points can you score in a minute?
  • Repeat the game for a minute - Can you beat your previous score?
  • Swing arm straight back and follow through
  • Roll the ball underarm
  • Keep your arm straight
  • Keep score of the game
  • Aim roll towards the cone
To make activity harder
  • Move further away from the cone
To make activity easier
  • Move closer to the cone
  • Use two cones side by side - larger target to hit

GameHit the Stumps 1 - diagramStrand : ADDuration : 5 - 10Teaching Points
  • Split the class into groups of 3/4
  • Each group requires 1 ball and 1 set of stumps
  • 1 child is the Wicket Keeper and the others are Fielders. Set up the activity as shown in the diagram
  • The Fielders aim to throw the ball from their cone to hit the stumps
  • The Wicket Keeper collects the ball and returns it to the next Fielder to have a go
  • Keep rotating the role of Wicket Keeper to ensure everyone has a go at the activity
  • Ask the children to remember how many times they hit the stumps
  • Use underarm throw - more accurate
  • Keep arm straight
  • Swing arm straight back
  • Swing arm straight through towards the target (stumps)
  • Accuracy before speed of throw
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Move the Fielders further back away from the stumps
  • Use underarm throw - more accurate
  • Swing arm straight through towards the target (stumps)
  • Swing arm straight back
  • Keep arm straight
  • Introduce more power into the throw for the increased distance
To make activity harder
  • Remove two stumps leaving only one stump to aim at - smaller target to aim at
  • Move the group further away from the wicket
To make activity easier
  • Add a second set of wickets to hit - larger target to hit
  • Move the group nearer to the wicket
