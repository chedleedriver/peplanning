RoutineGymnastics Activities: Unit 6 Task 2Strand : AD SA EIDuration : 15 - 25Teaching Points
  • Each child requires a space to work in on their own
  • Ask the children to create a simple sequence on the floor and using mats with the following elements:
  •     A Start position
  •     Up to 4 elements e.g. Balance, roll, jump, body shape
  •     A Finish position
  • Give the children time to develop and practice their sequence - encourage a smooth transition between shapes and actions
  • Once they have developed their sequence they should transfer it to a combination of floor, mats and apparatus e.g. Move from the floor to finish on apparatus, or move from apparatus to finish on the floor
  • The children should work in pairs to combine their sequence and perform their new, longer sequence, as a pair
  • Adaptations and variations on the task
  • Ask the children to:
  •     Vary the places in which they perform movements or actions, e.g. through, across, along, between, around large or small apparatus or mats
  •     Use lower, separate apparatus and link fewer actions in the sequence - this will make the task easier
  •     Use higher, linked apparatus and link more actions in the sequence - this will make the task harder
  •     Perform the sequence using different apparatus
  • Explain the task and what the children need to do then let the children explore the activity for themselves
  • Let the children have a go at the activity
  • What skills do you need to use? Ask the children to think about this as they perform the task
  • Encourage the children to think about the skills and actions they have learnt throughout the unit
  • Experiment with different ideas, skills and actions you have practiced throughout the unit
  • Observe the children performing the task - are they able to perform it successfully?
  • Have the children improved since last time they attempted the task? Are they more successful?
  • Provide advice and guidance if necessary
  • SAFETY POINT: Ensure the children are competent to perform the necessary actions/skills. Limit the actions that can be performed where necessary
  • SAFETY POINT: Ensure any apparatus is set out correctly and safely and the children know the rules for using it

Warm DownCircle Stretch Sitting - diagramStrand : KUFHDuration : 2 - 5Teaching Points
  • Split the class into groups of 6
  • Give each group a ball and ask them to form a small circle
  • Each child should sit on the floor facing the centre of the circle
  • Ask the children to roll the ball around the group passing it to everyone in the group
  • Once they have completed 10 passes the children should stop the activity
  • Aim roll of the ball
  • Encourage a quiet calm environment
