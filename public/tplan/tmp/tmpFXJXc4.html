Teacher Notes
  • Throwing is an important skill in rounders and many other games. It is a skill a child will use when fielding
  • The aim of the lesson is to develop the throwing skills of the children
  • The children should focus on the underarm throw
  • The children should be introduced to the concepts of:
  • Aiming - throwing the ball in the correct direction
  • Accuracy - throwing the ball as close as possible to the intended target

Warm UpMake them reachDuration : 2 - 5 Teaching Points
  • The children are to work in pairs and should stand facing each other 4/5 steps apart with 1 ball per pair
  • Ask the children to imagine their feet are 'glued' to the floor so they are unable to move
  • The children should throw their ball back and forth to each other
  • The child receiving the ball must catch it without moving their feet
  • The children should keep throwing and catching the ball
  • Make challenging but sensible throws
  • Can you catch with one hand?
  • Hands ready to catch
  • Keep your eyes on the ball
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Challenge the children to throw the ball to their partner so they have to reach/stretch to catch the ball
    To make activity harder
    • Use a smaller ball that is harder to catch
    To make activity easier
    • Use a larger ball that is easier to catch

    Skill DevelopmentThe Underarm ThrowDuration : 3 - 5Teaching Points
    • The children work in pairs with 1 beanbag between them
    • The children should stand 4 - 5 steps apart in a space and throw the beanbag back and forth to each other using an underarm throw
    • Keep the throwing arm straight
    • Swing the arm back
    • Swing the arm forward; keeping it straight
    • Release the beanbag as the arm moves past the body
    • Move quickly to collect your beanbag
    • Hands ready, eyes on the ball to catch it
    Diagram
    To make activity harder
    • The pairs must make more (10) successful throws and catches in a row
    To make activity easier
    • The pairs can make less (3) successful throws and catches in a row

    Skill DevelopmentMove ApartDuration : 5 - 10Teaching Points
    • The children are to work in pairs with 1 ball between them
    • They must start facing each other 2/3 steps apart
    • The child with the ball throws it to their partner, if they catch it successfully they take one step further apart
    • Keep throwing the ball back and forth
    • Every time a successful catch is made the child should take 1 step back
    • Cooperation
    • Good throws to partner
    • Be ready to catch - hands ready
    • Eyes on the ball
    • Watch the ball into the hands
    • Move in line to catch the ball
    ProgressionTeaching Points
    • Repeat the activity but 3 successful catches must be made before a step back is made
      To make activity harder
      • Catches must be made using one hand
      To make activity easier
      • Use a larger and/or lighter ball to increase success rate

      GameBeanbag BouleDuration : 5 - 10Teaching Points
      • Split the class into groups of 4 - each child requires 3 beanbags (the players beanbags should be different colours e.g. Player 1 has 3 red, Player 2 has 3 blue, Player 3 has 3 yellow, etc.
      • Each group requires a space to work in, marker spot and a cone - the activity should be set up as shown in the diagram
      • The children take turns to throw one of their beanbags from the marker spot to the cone - the aim is to get your beanbag nearest to the cone
      • Once all the beanbags have been thrown the child with the nearest beanbag scores 1 point - if they have the nearest 2 or 3 beanbags they score the appropriate number of points
      • The game is then played again
      • The children can play each game separately or keep a cumulative score of all games
      • Aim throws towards the target cone
      • Use an underarm throwing technique
      • Keep the arm straight, swing it back away from the target, swing it forward towards the target, release the beanbag as the arm moves past the body and follow through with the arm pointing towards the target
      • Take turns to throw
      • Throws must be made from the marker spot
      • Keep score of the game
      Diagram
      To make activity harder
      • Move the target cone further away
      To make activity easier
      • Move the target cone closer

      Warm DownCircle stretchDuration : 5Teaching Points
      • Split the class into groups of 6
      • Give each group 1 ball and ask them to form a small circle
      • Each child should stand with their feet shoulder width apart and reach down so their hands hang near the ground
      • The ball is then passed around the group - children should roll it around the circle
      • Ask the children to roll the ball around the circle 3 times in one direction and then 3 times in the opposite direction
      • Encourage children to keep their legs straight
      • Gradually decrease intensity
      ProgressionTeaching Points
      • After completing 10 passes, ask the children to walk around the area

        PlenaryTeaching Points
        What are the keys to a good throw?
        • Using a good technique
        • Aim the throw towards the target
        • The accuracy of the throw - how close it gets to the intended target
        • Correct power in the throw so it gets to the intended target

        PlenaryTeaching Points
        How can you make a throw more accurate?
        • Use an underarm throwing technique
        • Concentrate on aiming and accuracy instead of power
