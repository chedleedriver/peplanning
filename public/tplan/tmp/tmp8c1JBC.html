Teacher Notes
  • Balancing is the action of holding a still position for a set amount of time
  • The aim of the lesson is to let the children explore balancing and create balances for themselves using different body parts
  • Balancing can be described to the children as being frozen in position
  • The children should be introduced to the following terms:
  • Points of Balance - the parts of the body that are in contact with the ground to provide a base for the balance
  • Body Control - the ability to control and hold in position all the different body parts required to perform a balance
  • Stability - the firmness of a position, this requires good body tension to hold oneself in place
  • The children should be encouraged to explore balancing and the different parts of the body that can be used to balance on
  • Balances should be held for at least 5 seconds
  • Encourage the children to be creative and produce their own ideas and perform them for each other
  • Safety point: It is important that appropriate matting is used when performing balances

Warm UpHead-to-Toe Static Stretches - diagramStrand : KUFHDuration : 5 - 10Teaching Points
  • The children should find a space to work in and as a class perform each of the following stretches working from the top to the bottom of the body:
  •     Bring head forwards to look at toes and return to the middle
  •     Face head to one side of the body bringing chin to shoulder level - Return to the middle and repeat on the other side
  •     Ensure it is only the head that moves during stretches and that the head is always returned to the middle between stretches
  • Shoulders
  •     With arms held at the side of the body, circle one arm up, past the ear, behind the body and back to its starting position at the side of the body - Repeat with the other arm
  •     Keep arms straight and close to the side of the body, encourage the children to keep the rest of the body still as the arms move around the body
  • Wrists
  •     1. Place the hands together, interlocking the fingers with knuckles facing upwards - from wrists make a waving movement keeping hands together
  •     2. Place hands together palm to palm, interlocking the fingers - from wrists make a snake movement keeping hands together
  •     Sitting on the bottom with legs tucked up to chest, arms held around legs holding onto shins (Tuck Shape) - Rock slightly forwards and backwards on the bottom remaining in the tuck shape
  • Torso/Hips
  •     Standing with feet shoulder width apart, place hands on hips - make a circle shape with hips bringing them all the way forwards, to the sides and backwards
  •     Change direction after performing 3 circles and keep legs and back straight throughout the movement
  •     1. Sitting on the floor with legs apart and back straight (Straddle Shape) - Place arms straight up above head beside ears; turn the upper body and shoulder to face one foot; slowly reach towards toes bringing chest to legs and hands to feet with the aim of touching the toes; keep legs and back straight - Repeat on other side of the body
  •     2. Sitting on the floor with legs together and back straight (Pike Shape) - Place arms straight up above head and beside ears; slowly reach forward bringing chest to legs and hands to feet with the aim of touching the toes - Keep legs sand back straight
  • Ankles
  •     Sitting on the floor with legs straight out in front of body and back straight (the Pike shape) - point toes forward away from body = Happy Feet; then pull them back towards body = Sad Feet
  • Ensure each stretch is performed slowly
  • Ensure the children are performing the stretches/movements in a controlled manner
  • Hold the stretches for 8 - 10 seconds
  • Repeat each stretch 2 or 3 times

Skill DevelopmentIndividual balances - diagramStrand : AD SADuration : 5 - 10Teaching Points
  • Spread mats around the area - ensure they are spaced out safely
  • Ask the children to find a space on a mat to work on their own, ideally one or two children per mat
  • Teach the children the following individual balances - See notes below about each of the balances and relevant diagrams:
  •     Lying on back with body in a straight shape
  •     Lift head off the floor to look at toes - not too high
  •     Lift feet slightly off the floor about the same height as the head leaving just the back in contact with the floor - Ensure back is pushed into floor without a gap between the lower back and the floor
  •     Ask the children to bring their shoulders together at the front to create a rounded back shape
  •     Rest hands on top of legs
  •     To make it easier bend legs to start off with
  • Front Support:
  •     Start on hands and knees with hands flat on the floor facing forwards
  •     Keep stomach and back tight and in a straight line
  •     Place one leg backwards bringing it straight behind the body
  •     Bring the second leg backwards in a straight line and together with the other leg
  •     The Stomach, back, bottom and legs should all be in a straight line
  •     Shoulders should be directly over the hands with head looking at the floor
  •     Make sure bottom is not too high or stomach is to low, the body should be in a straight line
  • Arabesque:
  •     Standing in a straight position with feet together and arms straight out at either side of the body
  •     Place one leg behind the body - keeping it straight
  •     Slowly lift the leg off the ground to balance on one leg
  •     As the leg is raised lean slightly forward from the hips keeping as upright as possible
  •     Hold for three seconds before bringing the leg back to join the other leg and lift chest back up to a straight position
  •     Repeat on the opposite leg
  •     Sit on the bottom with the hands flat on the floor just behind the body - shoulder width apart
  •     Knees bent, feet flat on the floor - shoulder width apart
  •     Lift the bottom off the floor and raise the stomach so it points to the ceiling
  •     Hold this position with the arms and lower legs straight, the thighs and stomach in a straight line pointing to the ceiling
  • Encourage the children to control/stabilise all body parts when creating the balance
  • Encourage the children to hold the balances and keep the body tight
  • Encourage the children to perform the shapes to the best of their ability
  • Ensure adequate amount of space between each mat and each individual
  • Ensure the children use the correct technique
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Ask the children to create an individual balance with a number of points of contact
  • Keep varying the number of points of contact
  • Encourage the children to think of their own ideas
  • Encourage the children to explore balancing with a different centre of mass
To make activity harder
  • Decrease points of contact with the floor
To make activity easier
  • Increase points of contact with the floor

Skill DevelopmentPartner balances - diagramStrand : AD SADuration : 10 - 15Teaching Points
  • Spread mats around the area - ensure they are spaced out safely
  • Ask the children to get with a partner roughly the same height or size as them
  • Assign each pair to a mat
  • Give examples of some partner balances using one pair from the class to demonstrate. See notes and diagrams below
  • Ask the children to perform some individual balances with their partner mirroring their balance and some balances integrated with their partner:
  • Arabesque:
  •     Standing in a straight position with feet together and arms straight out at either side of the body
  •     Place one leg behind the body - keeping it straight
  •     Slowly lift the leg off the ground to balance on one leg
  •     As the leg is raised lean slightly forward from the hips keeping as upright as possible
  •     Hold for three seconds before bringing the leg back to join the other leg and lift chest back up to a straight position
  •     Their partner can then perform the same balance either at the side of them or in front of them
  • Front Support:
  •     Start on hands and knees with hands flat on the floor facing forwards
  •     Keep stomach and back tight and in a straight line
  •     Place one leg backwards bringing it straight behind the body
  •     Bring the second leg backwards in a straight line and together with the other leg
  •     The Stomach, back, bottom and legs should all be in a straight line
  •     Shoulders should be directly over the hands with head looking at the floor
  •     Make sure bottom is not too high or stomach is to low, the body should be in a straight line
  •     Ask the pair to perform the balance in different directions i.e. facing towards each other, facing away from each other, side-by-side
  • Straddle Support:
  •     One child sits on the floor
  •     Keeping the back upright, head forward, legs straight and feet wide apart to create a straddle shape
  •     Place arms on the floor to help balance
  •     Their partner should stand a couple of steps behind them, facing away from them
  •     Placing arms straight on the floor with fingers facing forwards place one leg straight behind the body to rest the top of their foot on their partners shoulder
  •     When balanced bring the other leg up straight to place the top of the foot on their partners other shoulder, creating a front support position
  •     Hold the balance for three seconds. Swap positions
  • T-Bridge:
  •     One child positions themselves on their hands and knees with fingers facing forwards, hands and knees shoulder width apart
  •     Keeping the back strong and straight in line with the bottom and shoulders
  •     Their partner stands a couple of steps away from the side of the child, facing away from them
  •     Placing the arms straight on the floor with the fingers facing forwards place one leg straight behind the body to rest the top of their foot on their partners lower back
  •     When balanced, bring the other leg up straight and place it on their partners lower back next to the leg already there, creating a front support position
  •     Hold the balance for three seconds. Swap positions
  • Children must work sensibly and safely when supporting each other
  • Cooperate with your partner
  • Encourage the children to control/stabilise all body parts when creating the balance
  • Encourage the children to hold the balances and keep the body tight
  • Encourage the children to perform the shapes and balances to the best of their ability
  • Ensure adequate amount of space between each mat and each individual
  • Ensure the children use the correct technique
  • Work together to perform the balances
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Ask the children to create a partner balance by performing another individual shape or a supported partner balance with certain body parts as points of contact i.e. hands and bottom
  • Encourage the children to use different parts of their bodies to balance on
  • Give the children more than one body part e.g. 1 hand and 1 foot
  • Encourage the children to explore balancing on different body parts
To make activity harder
  • Use alternative to feet as main point of contact
To make activity easier
  • Balance on named body parts

SequenceLink Together Balances - Individual - diagramStrand : AD SADuration : 10 - 15Teaching Points
  • Working on their own ask the children to create a sequence linking together balances they have learnt in the lesson (or in previous lessons)
  • The sequence should include:
  •     Linking together 3 individual balances with different points of contact - hands, feet and back
  • Ensure correct technique is used for the balances
  • Ensure the child is stable before balancing
  • Ensure the child is stable before balancing on the point of contact required
  • Ensure the children have practiced the basic individual balances first before trying to link them together
  • Keep eyes and head looking forwards
  • Number of balances in the sequence can be increased depending on children's progress
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Once the children have developed a routine ask some of the children to perform theirs for the rest of the class
  • This can be performed at the end of the lesson
  • Encourage the audience to give feedback - What did they like about the performance?
To make activity harder
  • Increase the number of balances to be linked
To make activity easier
  • Reduce the number of balances to be linked

  • Ask the children to stand in a semi-circle around the teacher
  • Show the children a stretch they have to copy
  • Start at the top of the body and work all the way down the body stretching the major body parts

  • Hold for 10-12 seconds
  • Hold still
  • Watch and copy

What different body parts can you balance on?
