Teacher Notes
  • A Sequence is a series of actions that are linked together - they can include any types of actions e.g. balances, shapes, travelling
  • The aim of the lesson is to teach the children what a sequence is and how to begin to perform one
  • Allow the children to explore and experiment by linking different shapes and actions together to perform a short sequence of 3 to 5 actions
  • A sequence can link different types of actions (e.g. 1 shape, 1 way of travelling, 1 balance) or a number of the same type of action (e.g. 3 different balances)
  • Encourage the following aspects to be considered when performing a sequence:
  • That the sequence is made up of the correct actions
  • How to link the actions together
  • How it looks - perform it to the best of your ability

Warm UpGet the Arms Loose - diagramStrand : KUFHDuration : 5 - 10Teaching Points
  • Spread marker spots around the area so there is one for each child to stand on in a space
  • The children all jog on their marker spots
  • Whilst jogging on the spot the children perform the following actions:
  •     Punch the air in front of them
  •     Punch the air high up above their heads
  •     Punch the air low down by their sides
  • Ask the children to stand still and perform arm circles on the spot - starting small and gradually increasing in size
  • Repeat arm circles backwards
  • Gradually increase the intensity of the activity
  • Keep jogging on the spot whilst performing actions
  • Listen carefully and follow the instructions

To make activity easier
  • Slow the activity down

Skill DevelopmentIndividual balances - diagramStrand : AD SADuration : 5 - 10 Teaching Points
  • Spread mats around the area - ensure they are safely spaced
  • Ask the children to find a space on a mat to work on their own, ideally one or two children per mat
  • Teach the children the following individual balances - See notes below about each of the balances and relevant diagrams:
  • Star balance:
  •     Standing up with feet apart
  •     Place arms straight out to the side of the body.
  •     Imagine legs and arms being pulled apart to create the shape of a star.
  •     Lift one leg off the floor and lean slightly to the opposite side
  •     Hold the balance for three seconds and bring foot back to floor.
  •     Repeat on the opposite leg
  •     Lying on front of the body in a straight shape
  •     Keep arms and legs straight
  •     Lift head, arms and legs off the floor keeping legs and feet together leaving just the stomach in contact with the floor - Only lift arms and legs slightly off the floor to ensure the child is not over bending their back too much
  • Knee Balance:
  •     Starting off kneeling up on one knee with the other leg placed straight out in front of the body
  •     Place arms straight out at the side of the body
  •     Slowly lift the leg in front of the body to hip height, keeping it straight
  •     Hold for three seconds and bring back down
  •     Repeat on the opposite leg
  • Encourage the children to hold the balances and keep the body tight
  • Encourage the children to perform the shape to the best of their ability
  • Ensure adequate amount of space between each mat and each individual
  • Ensure the children use the correct technique
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Ask the children to create an individual balance with a number of points of contact
  • Keep varying the number of points of contact
  • Encourage the children to think of their own ideas
To make activity harder
  • Introduce a bean bag for the children to balance on different body parts, i.e. foot, head arm.
To make activity easier
  • Balance on named body parts

SequenceLink Together Different Actions - Travelling and Balancing - diagramStrand : AD SADuration : 10 - 15Teaching Points
  • Ask the children to create a sequence linking together different ways of travelling and balances they have learnt in the lesson (or in previous lessons)
  • The sequence should include:
  •     Linking together 2 ways of travelling and 2 balances. They can be arranged and performed in any order
  • Encourage and highlight the use of good technique
  • Make sure the actions are performed with quality
  • Encourage smooth transition between the different actions
  • Encourage the children to show control over the movements and actions
ProgressionTeaching Points
  • Once the children have developed a routine ask some of the children to perform theirs for the rest of the class
  • Encourage the audience to give feedback - What did they like about the performance?
To make activity harder
  • Increase the number of elements to be linked
To make activity easier
  • Reduce the number of elements to be linked

Warm DownCircle Stretch - diagramStrand : KUFHDuration : 2 - 5Teaching Points
  • Split the class into groups of 6
  • Give each group a ball and ask them to form a small circle
  • Each child should stand with their feet shoulder width apart and reach down so their hands hang near the ground
  • The ball is then passed around the group - children should roll it around the circle
  • Ask the children to roll the ball around the circle 3 times in one direction and then 3 times in the opposite direction
  • Encourage children to keep their legs straight
  • Gradually decrease intensity

Plenary
What is a sequence?
  • A series of actions that are joined together e.g. a travel, a balance and a roll
  • The actions can be the same or similar (3 balances) or different actions
What actions can be used to create a sequence?
  • Any actions can be linked together e.g. Rolling. Balancing. Travelling. Key shapes
  • Encourage the children to think of the actions they used in the lesson to create their sequences
